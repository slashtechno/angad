I have not published a blog post for a significant amount of time. Because of this, I am considering remaking my website without a blog. The reason is, the blog posts (as of the time of writing), have to be manually added to the website in HTML. Hence, if I were to keep a blog, I would either build my own script that is an oversimplified version of Handlebars or use a static CMS such as Hugo.

However, there is one possibility. That being I solidify my understanding of HTML, CSS, and manual SEO to make a successful website with a blog. Will it be challenging? Yes. Will it be a better learning experience than a static CMS? Yes.

If I choose to build a website from scratch, maintenance will become a challenge. However, it will allow for a greater understanding of the code behind the website. Thus, I may attempt to remove the blog from the website homepage. If I have accomplished that, I may try to rebuild the website and temporarily discontinue work on the blog while I am improving my web development skills.

One more thing, I am aware the articles on my blog are quite short. If I revamp the website, I may focus it much less on the blog and more on showcasing my projects. This would allow for the website to show progress rather than unmaintained and short blog posts.

Thanks for checking out my website!
